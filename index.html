SOFTENING PARAMETER
Simulations 2 and 3 explored the impact of manipulating the softening length ("eps") and time step on the stability and accuracy of the particle system. Keeping the time step constant and varying "eps", it was found that reducing "eps" to 0.0001 resulted in a smoother and more accurate simulation. Set- ting "eps" to 1 demonstrated the circular dispersion of particles, emphasizing the need for a balanced softening length (figure 5 and figure 6). The simulations highlight the art of choosing the right "eps" value for accurate and realistic outcomes, as "eps" acts as a safety measure in calculations to avoid nu- merical problems when particles are close. The consequences of setting "eps" too high were illustrated, showing significant changes in the system with particles exhibiting unrealistic flows and fluctuations in circular velocity, cumulative mass, and surface density. In summary, these simulations emphasized the importance of selecting the softening parameter to achieve realistic and stable simulations. The results provided valuable insights into the dynamics of the simulated galaxy system under different softening lengths.
